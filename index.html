History

Founded as a French colonial settlement in 1674, Pondicherry remained under French control until 1954. The city preserves its colonial heritage through architecture, street names, and cultural traditions, making it a living museum of Indo-French history.

French Heritage

The French Quarter (White Town) showcases mustard-yellow colonial buildings, tree-lined boulevards, and charming cafÃ©s. French is still spoken, baguettes are baked fresh daily, and the architecture transports you to a Mediterranean village.

Culture & Climate

A unique blend of Tamil and French cultures creates vibrant festivals like Bastille Day and Pongal. The tropical climate offers warm weather year-round, with monsoons from October to December. Best visited from November to March.
